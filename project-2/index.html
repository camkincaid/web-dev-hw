Traditional Italian Carbonara Recipe

Over the past summer, while traveling in Europe, I experienced the best carbonara dish of my life in Rome, Italy. The flavor of the pecorino cheese combined with perfectly cooked homemade pasta and pieces of melting pancetta were literally mouth blowing. So, coming back to America, I was determined to find a recipe online which could resemble the classic Italian dish. This recipe is originally from allrecipes.
There's nothing more romantic than Italian food -Elisha Cuthbert

Ingredients

  • 2tsp olive oil
  • 1 pound guinciale (cured pork cheek), diced
  • 1 (16 ounce) package spaghetti
  • 3 eggs
  • 10Tbs grated percorino Romano cheese, divided
  • salt to taste
  • ground black pepper to taste

Directions

Prep 10 mins, cook 14 mins, ready in 29 mins
  1. Heat olive oil in a large skilled over medium heat, add guinciale. Cook turning occasionally, until evenly browned and crispy, 5 to 10 minutes. Remove from heat and drain on paper towels.
  2. Bring a large pot of unsalted salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring ocassionally until tender yet firm to the bite, about 9 minutes. Drain and return to the pot. Let cool, stirring occasionally, about 5 minutes.
  3. Whisk eggs, 5 tablespoons pecorino Romano cheese, and some black pepper in a bowl until smooth and creamy. Pour egg mixture over pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy and slighty cooled. Stir in guinciale. Top with 5 remaining tablespoons pecorino Romano cheese and more ground black pepper

Notes

Pecorino Romano
Italian cheese made from 100% sheeps milk, a sharper, salty alternative to parmesan
Guinciale
An italian cured meat product prepared from pork jowl or cheek. An alternative to guinciale is pancetta, an Italian bacon made of pork belly meat
Italian Pasta
